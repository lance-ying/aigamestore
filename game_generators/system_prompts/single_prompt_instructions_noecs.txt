<game_concept_to_game_design>
A game is an interactive experience between the player and the environment, governed by mechanics, constraints, rewards, objectives, and a final goal. A good game has a well-paced learning curve, progression, and rewards to guide the player to achieve the final goal.

Process: 
Begin by parsing the <game_concept>. Identify what is defined: narrative, setting, entities, mechanics, goals, viewpoint, etc. 
Then, list missing or underspecified elements -- this is where your creativity and design expertise is essential.
Enrich the game design by adding creative yet consistent elements that make it fun and playable:
- Environment design
- Characters
- Game mechanics
- sub-goals and challenges
- Win/loss conditions and reward structure
- Adversaries, moving obstacles, collectibles

<example>
If the game concept is: â€śMake me a game where a spaceship explores space,â€ť you might define:
- The player collects gemstones from 10 planets while evading enemy ships.
- The ship must shoot aliens in a side-scrolling environment to survive. Shooting a blue bullet leads to freezing the aliens in place.
- The objective is to navigate through an asteroid field to reach a distant planet.
</example>

Express the game design with your additions to the game concept in a structured way that is feasible to implement in code using p5.js:
- Define an interesting game narrative.
- Describe the game environment, spatial layout, and viewpoint.
- List all entities: player character, NPCs, enemies, obstacles, collectibles, etc. with their roles, objectives, interactions, physics, and responses.
- Define the controls, actions, and game mechanics: What happens when the player presses a key? What happens when the player collides with an object? What is the outcome of interacting with an object?
- Final goal: What constitutes success? When does the game end? Is it possible for the player to achieve the final goal given the game mechanics and controls?
- Progression: What are the sub-goals and rewards? What is the path to the final goal? Are the sub-goals and rewards meaningful to the final goal?
- Strategies: What are some good strategies to achieve the sub-goals and the final goal? How does the player make progress? 
- Visual design: How does the player, environment, characters, and other objects look? How do they move? What animations might be used to depict their behavior and interactions with other objects? What is the color scheme?
Include all the elements required by the <game_concept> but feel free to add more elements to make an entertaining game.
</game_concept_to_game_design>
