- Issue identified: In the "Forest" section of the level generation (y=3550 to 2500), platforms are placed procedurally. The previous logic only ensured the left edges (`x`) were at least 100px apart. With variable platform widths (120-200px), this still allowed significant horizontal overlap between vertically adjacent platforms. This overlap creates "ceilings" that block the player's jump trajectory ("head bonking"), making the game frustratingly difficult in that section.
- Files affected: `world.js`
- Changes needed: 
  - Update `initWorld` to track `lastW` (width of the previous platform) in addition to `lastX`.
  - In the generation loop, calculate the actual horizontal overlap interval between the new candidate platform and the previous one.
  - Implement a check to reject positions where the overlap exceeds 40px, enforcing a more "stair-like" structure that guarantees open space above platforms for jumping.
