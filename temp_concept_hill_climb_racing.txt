Players control a physics-based vehicle by tapping and holding a "gas" pedal on the right to accelerate and a "brake" pedal on the left to decelerate or reverse, navigating procedurally generated hilly terrain. Precise throttle and brake inputs are crucial to prevent the vehicle from flipping over or crashing. The game system incorporates a finite fuel supply that depletes over time, requiring players to collect fuel canisters scattered along the track to extend their run. Progression involves unlocking new vehicles like the Hill Climber or Monster Truck, and diverse stages such as the Moon or Arctic Cave, by achieving specific distance milestones or spending collected coins. The user interface features a large "gas" pedal button on the right side of the screen and a "brake" pedal button on the left for direct vehicle control. Content is delivered through a series of unique stages, each with distinct environmental physics and hazards, which are unlocked as players progress and achieve greater distances. The main objective is to drive the selected vehicle as far as possible on a given stage without running out of fuel or causing a crash that incapacitates the driver, Bill. Hill Climb Racing is a single-player offline game focused on individual skill and high-score chasing, with no direct multiplayer or social interaction features.

Play the original classic Hill Climb Racing! Race your way uphill in this physics based driving game, playable offline!

Meet Bill, the young aspiring uphill racer. He is about to embark on a journey through Climb Canyon that takes him to where no ride has ever been before. With little respect to the laws of physics, Bill will not rest until he has conquered the highest hills up on the moon!

Face challenges in unique hill climbing environments with a wide variety of cars to choose from. Earn points from daring tricks and collect coins to upgrade your car and travel further distances. Watch out though - Bill's neck is not what it used to be when he was a kid! And his good old gasoline crematorium will easily run out of fuel.
